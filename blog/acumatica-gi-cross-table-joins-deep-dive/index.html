Acumatica GI Cross-Table Joins — A Deep Dive

How joins work in Acumatica Generic Inquiries — left, right, inner, full, and the performance characteristics of each. With a worked example joining ARInvoices, Customers, and Branches.

John KihiuApr 18, 20267 min read

Most GI performance issues I have debugged came down to a join the developer added without thinking. Here is how to think about them.

The three join types Acumatica supports

  • Inner — only rows that match on both sides. Use when you only care about records that have a matching row on the other side.
  • Left — all rows from the left side, with matching rows from the right side (or NULLs). Use when you want all records regardless of match.
  • Right — the mirror of left. Rarely used because you can always flip the order.

Performance rules

  1. Each join doubles (roughly) the work. Don't add a join you do not need a field from.
  2. Join on indexed columns. Most Acumatica FKs are indexed; verify in the schema.
  3. Start the GI from the largest table and join down to the smaller ones.
  4. For the join to be a true inner join (not a 1-to-many explosion), make sure the relationship is unique on the joined side.
The most expensive GI in production Was one that joined ARInvoices to InventoryItem to Vendor to Warehouse to Branch — five tables, all left-joined. 2 million invoices × every combination. It took 4 minutes to load. The fix was a calculated column on ARTran that pre-resolved the warehouse, dropping the join to InventoryItem.

Wrapping up
